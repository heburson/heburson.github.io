Teaching Philosophy

I believe that everyone is capable of mastering the material covered in the courses I teach. I value questions, persistence, and risk-taking from students.

Outside of the specific content covered in each course, I find that math courses are a great place to practice the following skills:

  1. Breaking a problem into smaller parts.
  2. Organizing and defending an argument.
  3. Making connections between seemingly disparate topics.
